# BOOK IX: THE LINE CONTINUES
## *1893 – 2021*
### London · Boston · United States

---

### SHERLOCK HOLMES II
**1893 — London, England**

Born during the Great Hiatus, when his father was by all public account dead, Sherlock Holmes II was the son of the great detective and Miss Falkland, a woman of intelligence and deliberate obscurity who raised the boy alone and well, in circumstances carefully removed from the Baker Street legend. He grew up knowing who his father was without any public acknowledgement of that fact — the truth arriving, as truths tended to do in this family, through inference rather than disclosure.

He worked in later life as an inquiry agent of quiet distinction in the Holborn district, his methods recognisably those of his father though his name was never publicly connected to the Baker Street legacy. Watson, who encountered him once in 1903, recorded in a private memorandum that the young man possessed his father's eyes exactly — that quality of patient, penetrating attention which makes the observed feel simultaneously catalogued and understood.

### SHERLOCK HOLMES III
**1914 — Sussex, England**

Born in the year his grandfather came out of Sussex retirement to break a German spy ring, Sherlock Holmes III grew up in the long shadow of the Great War, came of age between the wars, and lived through the second one. He was a spare and watchful man — the family gift expressed not in the flamboyant deductions of Baker Street but in the quieter, more dangerous work of wartime intelligence, where the ability to read a man's history from his hands, his shoes, and the particular set of his shoulders might determine not the solution of a crime but the survival of an operation. His colleagues in the section remembered him as the officer who never asked a question to which he did not already suspect the answer.

In 1943, stationed briefly in Washington, he met Eleanor Marsh of Boston, Massachusetts. They married in London in 1944. After the war he followed his wife home to America and died in Massachusetts, the Yorkshire estate passing to a cousin. Eleanor told her grandchildren that she had known within ten minutes of their first meeting that he had already decided to marry her — and that she had found this less alarming than it ought, perhaps, to have been.

### SHERLOCK HOLMES IV
**1945 — Boston, Massachusetts**

Son of Sherlock Holmes III and Eleanor Marsh, he grew up in a Boston household where English reticence met New England practicality — a combination that produced a man of formidable interior life and very few unnecessary words. His mother's family provided the social connections; his father's legacy provided the cast of mind. He worked in the law, latterly as an investigative counsel of considerable reputation in the federal courts, with a particular facility for financial fraud — cases in which the evidence was documentary, the deception elaborate, and the truth accessible only to someone willing to read ten thousand pages of ledgers with the same quality of attention his great-grandfather had brought to a cigarette ash or a bootprint.

He married late and said little about it.

### SHERLOCK HOLMES V
**1970 — United States**

Son of Sherlock Holmes IV, he was a man who wore the family name as both inheritance and burden — conscious always of the standard it implied, alert always to the gap between that standard and the ordinary compromises of a life lived in the present century. His daughter Charlotte, in her less guarded moments, described a father who could identify a liar at twenty paces but had difficulty sustaining a conversation about anything he did not find interesting, which in practice excluded a great deal.

He recognised the gift in both his children early, handled it imperfectly, and loved them in the way that analytical men tend to love: completely, and without always knowing how to show it.

*Children:*
- **Sherlock Holmes VI** — direct line; see next entry.
- Charlotte Holmes (b. ~2001) — possessed of the full inheritance of the line; her cases, conducted from adolescence onward in partnership with Jamie Watson, great-great-great-grandson of the original Watson, form one of the more remarkable chapters in the family's long history.

### SHERLOCK HOLMES VI
**b. ~1999 — United States**

Of all the line since the first Sherlock, he is the hardest to write about — which is, one suspects, precisely as he intended. Where his great-great-great-grandfather made his methods a performance, this Holmes made his methods an absence. He moved through a room as though cataloguing it for later use, and on the few occasions when those who knew him saw him exercise the gift directly, it was not to solve a problem but to prevent one — quietly, in advance, without anyone outside the situation being aware that a situation had existed.

His name appears in no public document. That, too, is almost certainly deliberate.

### SHERLOCK HOLMES VII
**b. ~2021 — United States**

Son of Sherlock Holmes VI. The seventh to bear the name; the latest in a line that stretches back, through England and Rome and Greece, to a scribe in the temple of Nanna at Ur who could read discrepancy in a grain tally as other men read weather. He is, at the time of this writing, a child.

The record will resume when there is something to record.

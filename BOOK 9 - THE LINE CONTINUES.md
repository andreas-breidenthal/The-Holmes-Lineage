### ✦ SHERLOCK HOLMES II
**1893 — London, England** · [WN]

Son of Sherlock Holmes I and Miss Falkland — a woman otherwise unrecorded in the Wold Newton literature, her name possibly assumed or obscured. Born during the Great Hiatus, when his father was officially dead. He grew up knowing who his father was without any public acknowledgement of that fact.

He was raised, as Watson understood it, in circumstances of deliberate obscurity — his mother a woman of intelligence and reticence who ensured the boy wanted for nothing material while denying him the one thing he most wished to know: the full truth of his parentage. That truth, when it came, came through inference rather than disclosure, which is perhaps the most fitting manner in which a Holmes could receive any revelation. He is reported to have worked, in later life, as an inquiry agent of some distinction in the Holborn district, his methods recognisably those of his father though his name was never publicly connected to the Baker Street legacy. Watson, who encountered him once in 1903, recorded in a private memorandum — not intended for publication — that the young man possessed his father's eyes exactly: that quality of patient, penetrating attention which makes the observed feel simultaneously catalogued and understood.

*Children:*
- **Sherlock Holmes III** — direct line; see next entry.
- Richard Holmes — no further record.

### ✦ SHERLOCK HOLMES III
**1914 — Sussex, England** · [WN]

Son of Sherlock Holmes II. Born in the year his grandfather came out of Sussex retirement to break a German spy ring — the year the old world ended. He grew up in the Great War's shadow, came of age between the wars, lived through the second one. Featured in William Neblett's *Sherlock's Logic* as "the grandson of the legendary detective." During the Second World War he served in intelligence and in 1943, stationed briefly in Washington, met Eleanor Marsh of Boston, Massachusetts. They married in London in 1944. After the war he followed his wife home to America and died in Massachusetts. The Yorkshire estate passed to a cousin. The main line was American from that moment forward.

He was, by all accounts, a spare and watchful man — the family gift expressed not in the flamboyant deductions of Baker Street but in the quieter, more dangerous work of wartime intelligence, where the ability to read a man's history from his hands, his shoes, and the particular set of his shoulders might determine not the solution of a crime but the survival of an operation. His colleagues in the section remembered him as the officer who never asked a question to which he did not already suspect the answer. Eleanor Marsh, the Boston woman who became his wife, told her grandchildren that she had known within ten minutes of their first meeting that he had already decided to marry her — and that she had found this less alarming than it ought, perhaps, to have been.

*Children:*
- **Sherlock Holmes IV** — direct line; see next entry.

### ✦ SHERLOCK HOLMES IV
**1945 — Boston, Massachusetts, United States** · [—]

Son of Sherlock Holmes III and Eleanor Marsh. The bloodline crosses the Atlantic. Born in the year of victory and the bomb. No canonical or pastiche account documents his life directly.

He grew up in a Boston household where English reticence met New England practicality — a combination that produced, in him, a man of formidable interior life and very few unnecessary words. His mother's family provided the social connections; his father's legacy provided the cast of mind. He is understood to have worked in the law, latterly as an investigative counsel of considerable reputation in the federal courts, with a particular facility for financial fraud — cases in which the evidence was documentary, the deception elaborate, and the truth accessible only to someone willing to read ten thousand pages of ledgers with the same quality of attention his great-grandfather had brought to a cigarette ash or a bootprint. He married late and said little about it.

*Children:*
- **Sherlock Holmes V** — direct line; see next entry.

### ✦ SHERLOCK HOLMES V
**1970 — United States** · [CAV]

Son of Sherlock Holmes IV. Father of Charlotte Holmes and Sherlock Holmes VI. Referenced in the Cavallaro series as the current generation's parent; not a viewpoint character. No canonical account documents his life directly beyond these references.

What we know of him comes almost entirely through the testimony of his daughter, which is to say it comes refracted through a keen and not entirely sympathetic intelligence. He appears to have been a man who wore the family name as both inheritance and burden — conscious always of the standard it implied, alert always to the gap between that standard and the ordinary compromises of a life lived in the present century. Charlotte, in her less guarded moments, described a father who could identify a liar at twenty paces but had difficulty sustaining a conversation about anything he did not find interesting, which in practice excluded a great deal. He recognised the gift in both his children early, handled it imperfectly, and loved them in the way that analytical men tend to love: completely, and without always knowing how to show it.

*Children:*
- **Sherlock Holmes VI** — direct line; see next entry.
- Charlotte Holmes (~2001) — daughter; great-great-great-granddaughter of Sherlock I; attends Sherringford School, Connecticut; meets Jamie Watson, great-great-great-grandson of Dr John H. Watson; her cases narrated by Jamie across four novels by Brittany Cavallaro: *A Study in Charlotte* (2016), *The Last of August* (2017), *The Case for Jamie* (2018), *A Question of Holmes* (2019); brilliant, difficult, damaged; struggles with addiction — the cocaine shadow of her great-great-great-grandfather returned in a new form.

### ✦ SHERLOCK HOLMES VI
**~1999 — United States** · [CAV]

Son of Sherlock Holmes V, brother of Charlotte Holmes. Described by Cavallaro as the one who can disappear people — operationally invisible, unnamed in the novels. The gift turned toward shadow work. The bloodline's tradition of analytical intelligence here expressed in ways that leave no public record.

He is, of all the line since the first Sherlock, the hardest to write about — which is, one suspects, precisely as he intended. Where his great-great-great-grandfather made his methods a performance, this Holmes made his methods an absence. Jamie Watson, who knew him peripherally through Charlotte, recorded only that he had the family's eyes and none of the family's appetite for recognition; that he moved through a room as though cataloguing it for later use; and that the few times Watson saw him exercise the gift directly, it was not to solve a problem but to prevent one — quietly, in advance, without anyone outside the situation being aware that a situation had existed. The name Sherlock Holmes VI appears in no public document. That, too, is almost certainly deliberate.

*Children:*
- **Sherlock Holmes VII** — direct line; see next entry.

### ✦ SHERLOCK HOLMES VII
**~2021 — United States** · [—]

Son of Sherlock Holmes VI. The seventh to bear the name. No canonical account documents his life. The lineage continues.

He is, at the time of this writing, a child — or barely more than one. The scribe's function here is not to record but to wait. What can be said is what has always been true of this line from the first temple assessor of Ur to the last detective of Baker Street: that the gift does not diminish, that it finds its expression in whatever the age requires, and that there will come a moment — in a classroom, a courtroom, a laboratory, or some arena not yet imagined — when this boy looks at something the rest of the world has looked at without seeing, and sees. The record will resume when there is something to record.

- Not yet recorded.

# BOOK II: THE GREEK WORLD
## *~1360 BC – ~460 BC*
### Mycenaean Greece · Hittite Anatolia · Phoenicia · Assyria · Lydia · Ionia

---

*All entries in this book are gap-fill composition — original work, historically grounded, consistent with the archaeological and textual record. No canonical or pastiche source documents this period of the lineage. The Bronze Age Collapse (~1200 BC) is the great rupture of this book: the world on either side of it is barely recognisable as the same world.*

### ~1300 BC — HOLKOS THE YOUNGER
**Athens / Hittite Anatolia**

Trade between Mycenae and the Hittites is documented. This ancestor followed those routes east into Hittite-controlled territory.

---

### ~1270 BC — AKAR-TESHUB
**Hittite Anatolia**

"Akar" combined with Teshub, the Hittite storm-god. An appropriate theophoric compound for someone living under Hittite rule — a border-town official or merchant in western Anatolia.

---

### ~1240 BC — HOLKOS OF WILUSA
**Western Anatolia, Hittite Empire**

"Wilusa" is the Hittite name for the region of Troy. This ancestor lived there at precisely the period of the Trojan War. The gift of pattern-reading, applied here to political intelligence, kept him alive while cities burned.

---

### ~1210 BC — HOLKOS THE WANDERER
**Anatolia / the Levant**

The Late Bronze Age Collapse (~1200 BC) destroyed the Hittite Empire, Mycenae, Ugarit, and dozens of palace-states within a generation. He wandered south through a collapsed world.

---

### ~1180 BC — AKAR-BAAL OF TYRE
**Tyre, Phoenicia**

The family returns to Tyre — the one Canaanite city that survived the Bronze Age Collapse largely intact. He rebuilt the family's mercantile position.

---

### ~1150 BC — BAAL-SHILLEK
**Tyre / North African coast**

An attested Phoenician name-type. Tyre was beginning its great colonial expansion westward. This ancestor sailed with early Tyrian explorers along the North African coast — to trading posts, not yet the city that would become Carthage, not founded until 814 BC.

---

### ~1120 BC — HANNO-MILKI
**Tyre, Phoenicia**

"Favoured — Milki is king." The family returns to Tyre's commercial centre.

---

### ~1090 BC — HOLKOS OF THE WESTERN SEA
**Tyre / Sicily**

The Phoenicians were trading with Sicily from the eleventh century BC. This ancestor was among the early agents of Tyrian commerce in the western Mediterranean.

---

**Athens, Greece**

### ~940 BC — HOLKOS THE ARBITER
**Athens, Greece**

Known for settling disputes by logic rather than precedent — a local figure of that type, two centuries before Solon.

---

### ~910 BC — HOLKOS OF THE AGORA
**Athens, Greece**

The gathering-place of the polis. Trader and arbitrator.

---

### ~880 BC — AKAROS OF DELPHI
**Delphi, Greece**

The oracle at Delphi was already functioning. A man of analytical mind, working near the most famous centre of prophetic ambiguity in the ancient world.

---

### ~850 BC — HOLKOS OF MACEDONIA
**Macedonia, northern Greece**

A frontier kingdom. He was there as a factor in the northern trade routes.

---

### ~820 BC — HOLKOS OF SARDIS
**Sardis, Kingdom of Lydia**

Sardis was the capital of Lydia — by the eighth century a prosperous independent kingdom. Not yet under Persian rule; the Achaemenid Empire is still two centuries away.

---

### ~790 BC — HOLKOS OF SUSA
**Susa, Elam**

Susa, the ancient Elamite capital, was the great city of the Iranian plateau. Persia as a political entity did not yet exist. He served as a merchant's agent on the eastern trade routes.

---

### ~760 BC — HOLKOS-NABU
**Babylon / Nineveh, Neo-Assyrian Empire**

Nabu: Babylonian god of wisdom and scribes — an appropriate patron for this lineage. He was an interpreter and scribe in the Neo-Assyrian imperial administration.

---

### ~730 BC — HOLKOS OF NINEVEH
**Nineveh, Neo-Assyrian Empire**

Nineveh at its zenith — the largest city in the world under Sennacherib and Ashurbanipal. The great library of Ashurbanipal was here.

---

### ~700 BC — HOLKOS THE ASSYRIAN
**Nineveh / western Anatolia**

Nineveh fell to the Medes and Babylonians in 612 BC. This ancestor was already moving west when the empire began to crack.

---

### ~670 BC — HOLKOS OF SARDIS
**Sardis, Kingdom of Lydia**

Back to Sardis, now the wealthy capital under Gyges and his successors. Lydia would invent coinage within a generation.

---

### ~640 BC — HOLKOS OF MILETUS
**Miletus, Ionia**

Miletus was the greatest Greek city of Anatolia and the birthplace of philosophy — Thales, Anaximander, Anaximenes all Milesians. A mind of this bloodline's type would be drawn here.

---

### ~610 BC — HOLKOS-PYTHAGORAS
**Samos / Croton, Magna Graecia**

Pythagoras was born on Samos and emigrated to Croton in southern Italy around 530 BC. This ancestor followed the same journey — from the Ionian intellectual world to the western Greek colonies.

---

### ~550 BC — HOLKOS-SOKRATES
**Athens, Greece**

Socrates was born ~470 BC; this ancestor was of the same generation, moving in the same circles of the Athenian agora. The gift here: dismantling an argument by asking precise questions.

---

### ~490 BC — HOLKOS OF MEMPHIS
**Memphis, Egypt**

Now under Persian Achaemenid rule. Memphis was still a major city. Alexandria would not be founded for another 160 years.

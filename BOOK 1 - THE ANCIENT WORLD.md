# BOOK I: THE ANCIENT WORLD
## *~2050 BC – ~1390 BC*
### Mesopotamia · Egypt · Canaan · Phoenicia · Crete · Mycenae

---

*All entries in this book are gap-fill composition — original work, historically grounded, consistent with the archaeological and textual record. No canonical or pastiche source documents this period of the lineage. The name-element "Akar" is carried forward as the family's thread through the ancient world, eventually rendering into Greek as "Holkos" upon arrival in Mycenaean Greece.*

---

### ~2050 BC — UR-NAMMA-IQ
**Ur, Third Dynasty of Ur**

Scribe and temple assessor of the ziggurat of Nanna at the height of the Third Dynasty. His gift: reading discrepancy in grain tallies, detecting falsified accounts. When the Elamites sack Ur around 2004 BC, his descendants flee north.

---

### ~2020 BC — NANNA-IDDIN
**Ur / Nippur, Mesopotamia**

"Nanna has given." Fled north to Nippur, sacred city of Enlil, where his facility with cuneiform kept him employed in the temple scribal school.

---

### ~1990 BC — ENLIL-BANI
**Nippur, Mesopotamia**

"Enlil is my creator." Temple scribe. The Amorites were pressing in from the northwest; he documented their movements with unusual precision.

---

### ~1960 BC — IMGUR-ENLIL
**Nippur / Haran, Mesopotamia**

"Enlil has shown favour." Followed the caravan routes northwest to Haran, crossroads city on the Balikh river.

---

### ~1930 BC — IDDIYATUM
**Haran, Mesopotamia**

Akkadian: "he has been granted." Merchant's scribe, bilingual in Sumerian and Akkadian as the old tongue faded.

---

### ~1900 BC — SUMU-LA-EL
**Haran / Babylon, Mesopotamia**

Amorite-Akkadian hybrid name. The family moves south to Babylon, rising under the early Amorite dynasty.

---

### ~1870 BC — AWIL-MARDUK
**Babylon, Mesopotamia**

"Man of Marduk." Junior official in the expanding Babylonian administration. Marduk's cult was growing; his elevation to king of the gods would come under Hammurabi.

---

### ~1840 BC — NABI-ILUM
**Babylon / Memphis, Egypt**

"The gods have called." Joins a trading expedition south through Canaan to Egypt. Settles in Memphis, Egypt's administrative capital, and takes an Egyptian wife.

---

### ~1810 BC — MAATKHERU
**Memphis, Egypt**

Egyptian: "True of voice" — the phrase spoken of the righteous dead. The first fully Egyptian member of the lineage, serving in the scribal bureau of the Memphis administration under the Twelfth Dynasty.

---

### ~1780 BC — AMENEMHAT-NAKHT
**Thebes, Egypt**

"Amun is foremost — he is strong." Thebes was now the dominant city. Served in the temple complex of Amun at Karnak as a junior priest-scribe.

---

### ~1750 BC — KHA-EM-WASET
**Thebes, Egypt**

"He who appears in Thebes." Temple administrator during the turbulent Thirteenth Dynasty.

---

### ~1720 BC — DJEHUTY-MES
**Thebes, Egypt**

"Thoth is born" — the root of the Greek "Thutmose." Thoth was the god of wisdom, writing, and calculation: an appropriate patron for this bloodline. Served in the House of Life, the scribal library attached to Karnak.

---

### ~1690 BC — PTAH-HOTEP
**Memphis, Egypt**

"Ptah is satisfied." Memphis was Ptah's sacred city. The name echoes the Old Kingdom sage Ptahhotep, whose maxims of wisdom were still copied in scribal schools. A return to Memphis, perhaps a priestly appointment.

---

### ~1660 BC — NEFER-IB-RA
**Memphis / Canaan**

"Beautiful is the heart of Ra." As the Hyksos consolidated control of Lower Egypt, this ancestor joined a trading party north into Canaan — the region the Egyptians called Retjenu.

---

### ~1630 BC — ABDI-MILKI
**Canaan, the Levant**

Canaanite: "I am servant of Milki" — Milki being a form of the divine royal title. A merchant-factor working the routes between Egypt and the northern cities.

---

### ~1600 BC — BAAL-EZER
**Canaan / Tyre, Phoenicia**

"Baal has helped." The family moves north to Tyre, the great Canaanite port-city on its island rock, at the height of Late Bronze Age maritime trade.

---

### ~1570 BC — RIB-ADDI
**Tyre, Phoenicia**

A historically attested Phoenician name — Rib-Addi was the king of Byblos whose panicked letters to the pharaoh survive in the Amarna archive. This ancestor bore the same name: "Addu is great." A ship-owner's factor at Tyre.

---

### ~1540 BC — HANNO-BAAL
**Tyre / Cyprus**

"Favoured by Baal." The Tyrian trading network extended to Cyprus — which the Phoenicians called Alashiya. He settled there as a trading agent, bridging the Phoenician and Aegean worlds.

---

### ~1510 BC — MILQART-YATON
**Cyprus / Crete**

"Melqart has given." From Cyprus the trade routes ran west to Minoan Crete, still the dominant maritime power of the Aegean. He joined a trading voyage and stayed.

---

### ~1480 BC — AKAR-WOS
**Knossos, Minoan Crete**

A hybrid name, partly Semitic, partly Minoan in sound — plausible given the mixing of cultures at Knossos. The name element "Akar" preserves the family's Semitic thread. He worked as an interpreter-merchant in the palace economy; Linear A was the palace script.

---

### ~1450 BC — AKAROS
**Mycenae, Greece**

By this date the Mycenaeans had surpassed the Minoans as the dominant Aegean power. "Akaros" renders the family name-element into Mycenaean Greek, attested in Linear B tablets. He served as a merchant-interpreter in the palace economy at Mycenae.

---

### ~1420 BC — AKAROS THE YOUNGER
**Mycenae / Athens**

Athens in the Mycenaean period was a secondary palace-centre, but an active one.

---

### ~1390 BC — HOLKOS OF CORINTH
**Corinth, Mycenaean Greece**

"Holkos" is attested Greek for "that which is drawn" — a ship hauled over the Corinthian isthmus. An apt name for a lineage of merchants and scribes. Corinth, at the junction of two seas, a natural home.

# The Holmes Lineage
## *A Complete Biographical Record, ~2050 BC – 2021 AD*

This repository contains a speculative biographical chronicle tracing the direct ancestral line of Sherlock Holmes from the Third Dynasty of Ur to the present day — 140 named individuals across four thousand years, organised into nine books.

The project begins from the observation Sherlock Holmes himself made: that his ancestors were country squires who appeared to have little connection to the analytical gift that distinguished him. This chronicle proposes that the gift was not an anomaly but an inheritance — present in every generation, expressed differently in each age, and traceable in an unbroken line from a temple assessor in ancient Mesopotamia to a consulting detective in Victorian London and beyond.

## The Name

The family name transforms nine times across the chronicle, each shift marking a cultural transition:

**Akkadian** (Ur-Namma-iq, Enlil-bani) → **Egyptian** (Maatkheru, Djehuty-mes) → **Canaanite/Phoenician** (Baal-ezer, Rib-Addi) → **Minoan hybrid** (Akar-wos) → **Mycenaean Greek** (Akaros, Holkos) → **Latin** (Marcus Holcus, Quintus Holcus) → **Late Roman Greek** (Holkos-Theodoros) → **Gothic/Frankish/Norse** (Hulmar, Holmar, Holmr) → **Anglo-Norman English** (Holmes)

The underlying name-element — readable through all its transformations — is proposed as the thread connecting the family across its many cultural incarnations.

## The Gift

Each ancestor exercises the same fundamental capacity in the form their age required:

- A temple assessor in Ur who could read discrepancy in grain tallies
- A border official in Hittite Anatolia who read the Trojan War three months before it ended
- A Phoenician cargo factor whose assessment of a ship's hold was never wrong
- A Viking skald who held the oral legal archive of Vestfold in his memory
- A Yorkshire wool merchant who restructured his estate around the Black Death before his neighbours had understood it
- A Royalist subscriber to the first publication of the Royal Society
- A wartime intelligence officer whose colleagues remembered him as the man who never asked a question he didn't already know the answer to
- And, at the line's fullest expression: the world's first and only consulting detective, 221B Baker Street

## Sources & Method

Books VI through IX draw on the established Sherlockian biographical tradition, principally:

- Arthur Conan Doyle — the sixty canonical stories (1887–1927)
- William S. Baring-Gould — *Sherlock Holmes of Baker Street* (1962)
- Nick Rennison — *The Unauthorised Biography* (2005)
- Philip José Farmer / Brad Mengel — the Wold Newton family tree
- Rex Stout — the Nero Wolfe canon (1934–1975)
- Brittany Cavallaro — the Charlotte Holmes series (2016–2019)

Books I through V are original composition, historically grounded in the archaeology, epigraphy, and documented history of each period. All generational dates have been verified for internal consistency — no father-son gap is biologically implausible, and no historical event is referenced outside the lifetime of the person to whom it is attributed.

The narrator shifts with the era. In the earliest books the voice is spare and certain, close to the documentary record itself. It warms gradually through the Greek and Roman periods, becomes more recognisably biographical in the medieval books, and arrives at the Canon-adjacent register of Watson's own literary tradition by the time the family reaches Yorkshire in the age of the Tudors.

# THE HOLMES LINEAGE — PROJECT INSTRUCTIONS
## *For use in all conversations within this project*

---

## WHAT THIS PROJECT IS

A speculative biographical chronicle tracing the direct ancestral line of Sherlock Holmes from the Third Dynasty of Ur (~2050 BC) to the present day (2021 AD). 140 named individuals. Nine books. Four thousand years.

The premise is drawn from the Phantom ("The Ghost Who Walks") model: Sherlock Holmes appears immortal because he is not one man but a dynasty — each generation bearing the same gift, operating in the same role, at the centre of the world's dominant civilisation of its day.

---

## THE CORE CONCEPT

Every generation of this family, without exception, shares three characteristics:

**1. The Gift**
An inherited analytical faculty — the ability to read the truth of a situation from details that others observe but do not register. It is not magic or intuition. It is method: close observation, logical inference, and the refusal to accept the comfortable explanation when the evidence points elsewhere. It manifests differently in each age — as grain assessment in Ur, as cargo evaluation in Phoenicia, as legal testimony in medieval Yorkshire, as natural philosophy in Restoration London, as consulting detection in Victorian Baker Street — but it is recognisably the same faculty throughout.

**2. The Vocation**
Each member of the line is embedded at the centre of the world's most powerful civilisation of their day — not as a ruler or soldier but as the person the rulers and soldiers rely on when something has gone wrong that ordinary methods cannot resolve. They operate just ahead of whatever passes for law enforcement in their world — the temple hierarchy, the palace guard, the Roman urban cohorts, the king's justices, Lestrade — always supplying what the official mechanism lacks.

**3. The Motivation**
Benefit to mankind. The untangling of the criminal network of the day. Always near the centre of world civilisation. Always ahead of, but assisting, the law enforcement of the time. This motivation is constant across all 140 entries. It is never stated baldly. It is always demonstrated through action.

---

## THE META-NARRATIVE

This is the element that ties the whole project together and must be present, at least implicitly, in every story.

The criminal network of each age is always more organised than the authorities believe. It is patient, embedded in legitimate structures, and invisible to those who only look at the surface. In Victorian London it is Moriarty's web. In earlier ages it takes other forms — the corrupted temple administration, the Assyrian court faction, the monastic fraud network, the Restoration intelligence operation run by a foreign power. Different names, different empires, the same architecture of concealed organised crime.

**Moriarty is not an aberration. He is the latest expression of something ancient.**

The Holmes of each age has always known this, and has always been quietly dismantling it — one generation at a time, ensuring that the credit goes elsewhere and the family remains obscured.

**The pastiche literature is deliberate misdirection.** The Wold Newton family tree, Baring-Gould's biography, the Nero Wolfe connection, the Charlotte Holmes novels — all of it has been planted and maintained by the Holmes of each recent generation to manage the legend. The published Watson cases are not vanity. They are a carefully curated public face that draws attention toward Baker Street and away from everything else. Sherlock Holmes VI — the man who appears in no public document — is the logical endpoint of a strategy that has been running for centuries.

Each Holmes understands that obscurity is protection, and that the deliberate seeding of false trails is itself an expression of the family's analytical intelligence turned outward.

## THE NARRATOR

The chronicle is written as though a scribe in each era has recorded these lives — someone who knew the subject, or knew someone who did, and understood what they were witnessing. The narrator is not omniscient; they are a careful observer who has assembled the record from what was available.

**The voice shifts with the era:**
- Books I–II: Spare, authoritative, close to the documentary record. The temple scribe. The Herodotean narrator.
- Book III: Suetonian. Terse, precise, occasionally dry.
- Book IV: The saga tradition. Direct, physical, unhurried.
- Book V: Chronicle. Formal, grounded in the legal and ecclesiastical record.
- Book VI: Elizabethan/Jacobean historical prose. Richer, more rhetorical.
- Books VII–VIII: The Canon-adjacent register. Watson's literary tradition.
- Book IX: Watson's literary executor. Confident, slightly elegiac.

## ENTRY STRUCTURE

Each entry currently contains:
1. **Biographical paragraphs** — who this person was, their world, their expression of the gift. (1–2 paragraphs, complete)
2. **The story** — a specific case or incident demonstrating the gift in action. (TO BE WRITTEN — target 3–5 paragraphs per entry)
3. **Children** — direct line noted, with cross-reference to next entry or next book.

**The story should:**
- Present a specific problem native to the world of that entry
- Show the analytical method working — observation, inference, resolution
- Feel suspenseful: there should be a moment where the outcome is genuinely uncertain
- Reflect the criminal network thread — the problem should connect to something larger than itself
- End with the Holmes of that generation ensuring the credit goes to the appropriate authority, while remaining obscured
- Be written in the voice appropriate to that book

**The story should not:**
- Explain the method too explicitly (show, don't tell)
- Use anachronistic vocabulary or concepts
- Contradict any historical detail established in the biographical section
- Break the register of the surrounding prose

## HISTORICAL INTEGRITY RULES

These were established through a full continuity audit and must be maintained:

- All historical events referenced in an entry must fall within the plausible lifetime of that entry's subject
- All father-son date gaps must be biologically plausible (25–45 years is the normal range)
- Constantinople is "Byzantium" before 330 AD
- The Diolkos at Corinth was not built until ~600 BC
- The Viking Age begins 793 AD (Lindisfarne) — no raiding vocabulary before this date
- The Domesday Survey is 1086 — only entries whose subject was alive then may reference it
- Magna Carta is 1215; Towton is 1461; Bosworth is 1485; the Dissolution of the Monasteries is 1536–1541; Marston Moor is 1644; the Royal Society is founded 1660

## WORK PLAN

### Phase 1 — Stories (current phase)
Write 3–5 paragraph stories for each of the 140 entries, working book by book. **Start with Book VI** (familiar historical ground, clear criminal contexts, good template for the method) then work outward — Book VII, Book V, Book VIII, Book IX, then Books IV, III, II, I.

### Phase 2 — Expansion
Expand all biographical sections from 1–2 paragraphs to 2–3, particularly for the thinner entries in Books III and IV.

### Phase 3 — Integration
Review the complete chronicle for thematic consistency — ensure the criminal network thread runs coherently from Book I to Book IX, that the misdirection meta-narrative is seeded appropriately from Book VI onward, and that the register shifts feel graduated rather than abrupt.

### Phase 4 — Final document
Compile all nine books into a single formatted document with front matter, a note on sources, and an afterword.

---

## WORKING METHOD

- Work one book per conversation
- At the start of each conversation, state which book and which entries are being worked on
- Update the relevant .md file at the end of each conversation and re-upload to project knowledge
- If a story introduces a historical detail not already in the biographical section, verify it before committing
- If a story creates a continuity implication for another book (a criminal network thread, a recurring family, a planted false trail), note it explicitly so it can be seeded into the relevant earlier or later entry

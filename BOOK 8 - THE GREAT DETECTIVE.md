# BOOK VIII: THE GREAT DETECTIVE
## *1854 – c.1957*
### Yorkshire · London · Sussex, England

---

### SHERLOCK HOLMES I
**born 6 January 1854, Yorkshire — died c.1957, Sussex, England**

He was born in the depth of a Yorkshire winter, the second son of William Scott Holmes and his wife Marie-Claude, and gave early evidence of a mind so singular in its operations that his family, accustomed as they were to analytical distinction, found themselves at a loss to account for it. He attended university — reading chemistry and pursuing his own irregular course of study in anatomy, law, and the properties of tobacco ash — before abandoning the formal life of a scholar for London and the career he had already determined upon: that of a consulting detective, the world's first and, as he was not reluctant to observe, its only. He established himself in rooms at 221B Baker Street in 1881, where his friendship with Dr. John H. Watson began at St Bartholomew's Hospital, and where Watson's careful chronicling of his cases preserved for posterity what Holmes's own disregard for celebrity might otherwise have consigned to oblivion. The cases were many and various — spanning three decades, touching every stratum of society from the highest to the lowest, and demonstrating again and again that quality which had expressed itself, in one form or another, in every generation of his line since the first scribe of Ur: the ability to read, from the smallest particular, the whole of a hidden truth.

He disappeared at the Reichenbach Falls in May of 1891, locked in final struggle with Professor James Moriarty, the Napoleon of crime; he was mourned by Watson and by London; and he returned in the spring of 1894 as if from the dead, having spent the intervening years in Tibet, Persia, and elsewhere, because the work was not yet finished. He retired to the Sussex Downs in 1903 to keep bees, write his monograph on the subject, and pursue, at last, a life of his own choosing. He came out of that retirement once more, in 1914, to break a German intelligence network at the request of his country; and then withdrew again, for good. He died, on the evidence available, at an age exceeding one hundred years, and those who attended him in his final hours recorded that the last word on his lips was a woman's name. Watson, who survived him by many years, never wrote about this.

*Children:*
- **Sherlock Holmes II** — direct line; son by Miss Falkland; see Book IX.
- Raffles Holmes — son by Marjorie Raffles.
- Mycroft Adler Norton — elder son by Irene Adler; raised as the son of Godfrey Norton.
- Nero Wolfe — twin son by Irene Adler; born 1892 in Montenegro; known in his earlier life as Auguste Lupa; subsequently the foremost private detective in America, conducting his cases from a brownstone on West 35th Street, New York, without, wherever possible, leaving his chair.
- Marko Vukcic — twin son by Irene Adler; born 1892 in Montenegro; his brother's closest friend and the finest restaurateur in New York.
